Directions

QUT has two main campuses, make sure you go to the Gardens Point Campus, next to the Botanic Gardens in Brisbane City.

The symposium will be in the Owen J. Wordsworth Room which is on Level 12 of S-Block.

Wi-Fi at QUT

QUT has free public wifi that you can login to here. Make sure you first register an account and password here. You'll need to verify your account via email.

Car parking at QUT

Parking is available at QUT Gardens Point campus. The closest parking facilities are in the Science and Engineering Centre (P block). All parking on campus incurs a fee.

Due to limited parking options, we encourage you to consider alternative transport such as public transport or taxi/ride-share services. For more information on parking, see here.

Public transport at QUT

Public transport information for buses, ferries and trains is available on the translink website. Use "QUT Gardens Point Campus" as the "end location".

  • Ferry: The QUT Ferry Terminal is a short walk from Z-Block.
  • Bus: Look for buses that stop on Alice Street in the City. Any buses that stop in the city will be a short walk to the Gardens Point Campus.
  • Train: Either get off at "South Bank" station and walk across the Goodwill Bridge, or get off at "Central" station and walk through the city.

You can pay for tickets using debit or credit cards, including those linked to the digital wallet of your smartphone. See here for details.

Accessibility at QUT

The Owen J Wordsworth room has an infrared System installed for hearing assistance. The rooms are easily accessible by wheelchair. There are toilets on the same level.

We welcome attendees with children. The nearest baby change facility is on Level 3 of X-block near the entrance near lifts. Amenities include armchair, sink and baby change station. We are happy to help you find this facility.

Places to stay near Gardens Point

There are lots of hotels in Southbank, which is just a short walk across the Goodwill Bridge (pedestrian bridge). There are also lots of hotels and apartments in the city, and this will be a short walk down George Street or through the botanical gardens.
